Privacy Policy

Last updated: 19 July 2026

Thank you for visiting the website of Free My Thoughts ("we", "us" or "our"). We are committed to protecting your personal information and your right to privacy. If you have any questions or concerns about this privacy notice or our practices with regard to your personal information, please contact us at marie@freemythoughts.co.uk.

This privacy notice describes how we might use your information if you visit our website at https://freemythoughts.co.uk or engage with us in other related ways — including any sales, marketing, or events. Please read it carefully, as it will help you understand what we do with the information we collect. If there are any terms you do not agree with, please discontinue use of our services.

1. What information do we collect?

In short: we collect personal information that you provide to us.

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide when you express an interest in our services, contact us, or use our contact form. Depending on how you interact with us, this may include your name, phone number, email address, and the details of your enquiry.

Payment data. Payments for our services are made by bank transfer. We do not collect or store card details, and no payment information is taken through this website.

Information automatically collected. Our hosting provider may automatically log limited technical information — such as your IP address and browser type — when you visit the website, for security and operational purposes. This does not reveal your specific identity and we do not use it to track you.

2. How do we use your information?

We process your information based on our legitimate business interests, the fulfilment of our contract with you, compliance with our legal obligations, and/or your consent. We use it to respond to your enquiries, deliver our services, request feedback, post testimonials (only with your consent), and — only if you have opted in — send you marketing communications, which you can opt out of at any time.

3. Will your information be shared with anyone?

We only share information with your consent, to comply with laws, to provide you with services, to protect your rights, or to fulfil business obligations. We may share data with third-party service providers who perform services on our behalf — our website host, our booking service (Calendly) and our contact-form service (Formspree). They cannot use your personal information for anything other than what we have instructed. Some of these providers are based in the United States; where that is the case, transfers are made under UK-approved safeguards. We do not sell, rent or trade your information.

4. Do we use cookies?

No. This website does not use cookies, analytics or tracking technologies. Third-party sites we link to (such as our Calendly booking page) may use their own cookies, which are governed by their own privacy policies.

5. How long do we keep your information?

We keep your personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes set out in this notice — generally no longer than 2 years after our last contact. The exception is invoices and payment records, which UK tax law requires us to keep for six years. When we have no ongoing legitimate business need to process your information, we will delete or anonymise it.

6. How do we keep your information safe?

We have implemented appropriate technical and organisational security measures designed to protect your personal information. However, no electronic transmission or storage technology can be guaranteed 100% secure, so transmission of personal information to and from our website is at your own risk.

7. Do we collect information from minors?

We do not knowingly collect data from or market to children under 18 years of age. If you become aware of any data we may have collected from children under 18, please contact us at marie@freemythoughts.co.uk.

8. What are your privacy rights?

Under UK data protection law you have rights including: the right to request access to and obtain a copy of your personal information; the right to request rectification or erasure; the right to restrict processing; and, where applicable, the right to data portability. You may also withdraw consent at any time. To make a request, contact us at marie@freemythoughts.co.uk.

If you believe we are unlawfully processing your personal information, you have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO): https://ico.org.uk.

9. Updates to this notice

We may update this privacy notice from time to time. The updated version will be indicated by an updated "Last updated" date and will be effective as soon as it is accessible.

10. How can you contact us?

If you have questions or comments about this notice, or wish to review, update or delete the data we hold about you, email us at marie@freemythoughts.co.uk.
