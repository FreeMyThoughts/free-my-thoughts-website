Coaching Disclaimer

All coaching services and communication delivered by Free My Thoughts, and the information on this website, are intended to help you identify the areas in your life and in your thinking that may be standing in your way.

Coaching is not professional mental health care or medical care, and it is not a substitute for counselling, psychotherapy, psychoanalysis or treatment for substance abuse. It is not intended to diagnose, treat or cure any mental health or medical condition. Coaching can sit alongside therapy, but it works best when major emotional and psychological wounds are already healing or healed.

If you're struggling to function or are in crisis, please speak to your GP — or call Samaritans free on 116 123, any time.

What to know before we work together

  • Your decisions remain your own. Comments and ideas offered in coaching are there to help you reach your goals. You remain responsible for your own choices, decisions and wellbeing, during and after our work together.
  • No promised outcomes. Where our work involves your career or business, we do not promise specific results — including increased clientele, profitability or business success.
  • Confidentiality. What you share in coaching is treated as confidential. The exceptions are those required by law: a risk of serious harm to you or to someone else, a safeguarding concern, or a court order requiring disclosure.
  • Technology. Email, phone and video calls are never perfectly secure; by using them you accept the confidentiality risks they carry.
  • Regulation. Coaching is currently an unregulated profession in the UK.

Our liability in connection with coaching services is as set out in our Terms and Conditions. If anything here is unclear, please ask — it matters to us that you begin coaching with clear, informed consent.
