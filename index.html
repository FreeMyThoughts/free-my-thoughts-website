About Marie

Hi, I'm Marie

A qualified coach, mum of two (three if you include my gorgeous dog, Saffy), lover of the great outdoors — and a recovered Imposter Syndrome addict. Addict?! I hear you say. Who in their right mind would be addicted to something they know holds them back and feels, well, let's be honest, really crappy?

But here's the truth: Imposter Syndrome shows up as an (unhelpful) way of trying to keep us safe. Like most people's, my nervous system is hard-wired for safety — it's a natural evolutionary response. Change and risk feel uncertain and scary, so in rides Imposter Syndrome on its white horse, valiantly trying to rescue us by encouraging us to play small.

I spent years in that state — shrinking myself to please others, avoiding risks, and berating myself with narratives I wouldn't have used on my worst enemy. It has taken years of inner work, self-discovery and training to be where I am today. Imposter Syndrome still rears its head now and then, but it's no longer in the driving seat. I am. My life has expanded, I'm running the business of my dreams, and I'm able to take the opportunities that come my way.

The truth

You are not alone

Imposter Syndrome, unlike everyday self-doubt, lives deep within us — at the level of identity and how we truly see ourselves. And it is rife: it's estimated that up to 58% of men and 70% of women regularly experience Imposter Syndrome — and those figures increase further still if you're from an ethnic minority background, or neurodivergent. Remember that next time those unhelpful thoughts insist the world and his wife have it all together and you're the only one who hasn't. Some of the colleagues and friends who seem so confident and sorted? They're more than likely dealing with it too.

Because Imposter Syndrome operates at such a deep level, the surface-level tactics people use to address it invariably don't work. That's why I dive deep with my clients: we address your programming at identity level, and I teach you the tools and strategies you need to create sustainable, lasting change.

It's a commitment on your part — and I only work with clients who are in a place where they're ready to do the real work. If you're looking for a quick and easy fix (spoiler: it doesn't exist), then I'm not the coach for you.
